In a serially connected circuit the voltage across is directly proportional to the resistance in the circuit. That is,

    \[ V\propto R \]

    .



    So,

    \[ \dfrac { V }{ { V }_{ total } } =\dfrac { R }{ { R }_{ total } } \]



    The total resistance

    \[ R \]

    in the circuit is

    \[ R=2+3+5= 10 \]

    ohm.



    Since, the resistance of the

    \[ 3 \]

    ohms resistor is only three tenth of the total resistance, the applied voltage is given as

    \[ \dfrac { 3 }{ 10 } \times 2 =\ 0.6 \]

    volt.



    Hence, the potential difference between the terminals of 3 ohm resistance will be 0.6 volt.
