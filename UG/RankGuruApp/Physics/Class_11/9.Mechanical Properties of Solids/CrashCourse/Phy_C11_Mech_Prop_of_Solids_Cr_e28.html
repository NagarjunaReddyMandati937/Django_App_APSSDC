    \[ F \]

    Force given by the boy


    \[ W \]

    weight


    \[ L \]

    length


    \[ \alpha= \]

    angular acceleration


    \[ a_{cm}= \]

    Linear acceleration of the center of the mass.


    Linear equation of motion from the free body diagram


    \[ W-F=(\dfrac{W}{g})a_{cm} \]

    . . . . . . . .(1)


    We know that,


    \[ W(\dfrac{L}{2})=I\alpha \]


    \[ \dfrac{WL}{2}=\dfrac{WL^2}{3g}\alpha \]


    \[ I=\dfrac{mL^2}{3}, m=\dfrac{W}{g} \]


    \[ \alpha=\dfrac{3g}{2L} \]

    . . . . . . . .(2)


    The acceleration of center of mass


    \[ a_{cm}=\dfrac{\alpha L}{2}=\dfrac{3g}{4} \]


    Substitute in equation (1), we get,


    \[ W-F=\dfrac{W}{g}\times \dfrac{3g}{4} \]


    \[ F=W-\dfrac{3W}{4}=\dfrac{4W-3W}{4} \]


    \[ F=\dfrac{W}{4} \]
