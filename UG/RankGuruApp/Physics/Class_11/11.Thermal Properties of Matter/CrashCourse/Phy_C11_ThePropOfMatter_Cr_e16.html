The transfer of thermal energy from one single body to another and from one particular temperature to other - high to low temperature is possible only under steady or unsteady conditions. In this question, there is steady-state which means temperature does not change with time, at point c the temperature would remain the same which is

    \[ Tc. \]


    \[ \left (\dfrac {\triangle Q}{\triangle t}\right )_{BC} = \left (\dfrac {\triangle Q}{\triangle t}\right )_{CA} \]


    \[ \Rightarrow \dfrac {kA(\sqrt {2}T - T_{C})}{a} = \dfrac {kA (T_{C} - T)}{\sqrt {2}a} \]


    Solve to get


    \[ \dfrac {T_{C}}{T} = \dfrac {3T}{\sqrt {2} + 1} \]


    Therefore, heat conduction takes place is equal to

    \[ \dfrac{3T}{\sqrt{2}+1}. \]
