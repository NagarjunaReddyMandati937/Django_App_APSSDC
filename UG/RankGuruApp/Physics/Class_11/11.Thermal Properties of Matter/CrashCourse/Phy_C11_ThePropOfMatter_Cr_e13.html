Given,


    Two metallic spheres

    \[ S_1,S_2 \]


    \[ S_1=3S_2 \]


    Let

    \[ m_1 \]

    is the mass of

    \[ s_1 \]

    and

    \[ m_2 \]

    is the mass of

    \[ s_2 \]


    So,


    \[ m_1=3m_2 ....1 \]


    Now, heat for

    \[ s_1= (dQ)=m_1\times dT \]


    where s is molar heat capacity, dT is change in temprature


    So,


    \[ \dfrac{dQ}{dT}=m_1\times s\times(\dfrac{-dT}{dt}) ....2 \]


    where dT is the change in time (-)sign indicates that this case in cooling.


    Similarly,


    \[ s_2=\dfrac{dQ}{dT}=m_2\times(\dfrac{-dT}{dt}) ....3 \]


    From equation 1,2 and 3 we get,


    \[ \dfrac{m_1}{m_2}=\dfrac{3}{1} \]
