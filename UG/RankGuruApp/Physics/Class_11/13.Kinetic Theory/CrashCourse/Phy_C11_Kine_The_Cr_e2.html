Heat at constant pressure,


    \[ \Delta Q=nC_P \Delta T \]

    . . . . . . .(1)


    Where,

    \[ n = \]

    no. of moles


    \[ C_P= \]

    molar heat capacity at constant pressure


    for diatomic gas, ,

    \[ C_P=\dfrac{7R}{2}, \ C_V=\dfrac{5R}{2} \]


    So,

    \[ \Delta Q=n(\dfrac{7R}{2})\Delta T \]

    . . . . . .(2)


    Change in internal energy at constant volume,


    \[ \Delta U=n C_V \Delta T \]


    \[ \Delta U=n(\dfrac{5R}{2})\Delta T \]

    . . . . .(3)


    ratio,

    \[ \dfrac{\Delta U}{\Delta Q}=\dfrac{n\dfrac{5R}{2}\Delta T}{n\dfrac{7R}{2}\Delta T}=\dfrac{5}{7} \]


    Hence,

    \[ 5/7 \]

    part of heat supplied is used to increase the internal energy. The correct option is D.
