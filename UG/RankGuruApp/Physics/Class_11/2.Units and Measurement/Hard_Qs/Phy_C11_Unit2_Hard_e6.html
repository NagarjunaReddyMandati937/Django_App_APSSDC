Let focal length of the mirror be

    \[ \ f \ \]

    cm.


    \[ \dfrac{1}{v} + \dfrac{1}{u} = \dfrac{1}{f} \]


    \[ \dfrac{1}{20.1} + \dfrac{1}{50.1} = \dfrac{1}{f} \]


    \[ \implies f = 14.3 \]


    As,

    \[ f = \dfrac{uv}{u+v} \]


    \[\therefore \ \]

    Taking log and differentiating, we get

    \[ \Delta f = f \bigg( \dfrac{\Delta u}{u} + \dfrac{\Delta v}{v} + \dfrac{\Delta u + \Delta v}{u+ v} \bigg) \]


    \[ \Delta f = 14.3 \bigg( \dfrac{0.5}{50.1} + \dfrac{ 0.2}{20.1} + \dfrac{0.5 +0.2}{50.1+20.1} \bigg) \]

    \[= 0.4 \]

    cm.


    Thus focal length of the mirror

    \[ = (14.3 \pm 0.4) \]

    cm.
