Using

    \[ x=A \sin \omega t \]


    For

    \[ x=A/2,\ sin \omega T_1=1/2 \]


    \[ \Rightarrow T_1=\dfrac{\pi}{6 \omega} \]


    For

    \[ x=A,\ \sin \omega (T_1+T_2)=1 \]


    \[ \Rightarrow T_1+T_2=\dfrac{\pi}{2 \omega} \]


    \[ \Rightarrow T_2=\dfrac{\pi}{2 \omega}-T_1 \]


    \[ =\dfrac{\pi}{2 \omega} - \dfrac{\pi}{6 \omega}=\dfrac{\pi}{3 \omega} \ \]


    \[ i.e., T_1 < T_2 \]



    Alternative method : In SHM, velocity of particle also oscillates simple harmonically. Speed is more near the mean position and less near the extreme position. Therefore the time taken for the particle to go from

    \[ 0 \]

    to

    \[ \dfrac{A}{2} \]

    will be less than the time taken to go from

    \[ \dfrac{A}{2} \]

    to

    \[ A \]

    .Hence

    \[ T_1 < T_2 \]
