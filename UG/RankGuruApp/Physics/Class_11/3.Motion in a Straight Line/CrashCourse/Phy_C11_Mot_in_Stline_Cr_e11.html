Let the mass of the system be

    \[ m \]


    Initial velocity of bus

    \[ u = V \]


    Final velocity of bus

    \[ v = 0 \]


    Acceleration of the bus

    \[ a =- \dfrac{F}{m} \]


    Using

    \[ \ v^2-u^2 = 2aS \]


    \[ \therefore \ 0-V^2 = 2 \times \bigg( \dfrac{-F}{m} \bigg) \times x \]


    \[ \implies \ V^2 = \dfrac{2Fx}{m} \]

    ..........(1)


    Now the mass of the system is increased by

    \[ 25 \]

    %


    \[\therefore \ \]

    New mass of the system

    \[ M = 1.25 m \]


    Acceleration of bus

    \[ a' = -\dfrac{F}{1.25m} \]


    Using

    \[ v^2-u^2 = 2a'x' \]


    \[ \therefore \ 0-V^2 = 2 \times \bigg( \dfrac{-F}{1.25m} \bigg) \times x' \]


    \[\implies \ V^2 = \dfrac{2Fx'}{1.25m}\]

    .......(2)


    From (1) and (2) we get

    \[ \dfrac{2Fx'}{1.25m} \ = \dfrac{2Fx}{m} \]


    \[ \implies \ x' = 1.25 x \]
