Initial upward velocity

    \[ u = 40 \ ms^{-1} \]


    Let after time

    \[ t \]

    the particle reaches the maximum height

    Using

    \[ v = u - gt \]


    \[ \therefore \ 0 = 40 - 10 t \]


    \[ \implies t =4\ s \]


    Thus after ,

    \[ 4\ s \]

    the particle starts the downward motion for another

    \[ t = 2\ s \]


    Distance traveled in upward motion

    \[ S_1 = ut - \dfrac{1}{2}gt^2 \]

    where

    \[ t = 4\ s \]


    \[ \therefore \ S_1 = 40 \times 4 - \dfrac{1}{2}10 \times 4^2 = 80 \]


    Distance travelled in downward motion

    \[ S_2 = u't + \dfrac{1}{2}gt^2 \]

    where

    \[ t = 2 \ s \]

    \[ \therefore \ S_2 = 0 + \dfrac{1}{2}10 \times 2^2 = 20 \]


    and

    \[ u' = 0 \]


    Total distance covered in

    \[ 6\ s \]

    :

    \[ S_T = 80 + 20 = 100 \ m \]


    Displacement in

    \[ 6\ s \]

    \[ S = ut - \dfrac{1}{2}gt^2 \]


    where

    \[ t = 6 \ s \]


    \[ S = 40 \times 6 - \dfrac{1}{2}10 \times 6^2 = 60 \ m \]
