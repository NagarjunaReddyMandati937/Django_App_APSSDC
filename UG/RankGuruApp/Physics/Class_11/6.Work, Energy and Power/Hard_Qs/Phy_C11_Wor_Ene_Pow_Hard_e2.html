given position vector of particle at time

    \[ t \]


    \[ \vec{r}=\left\{(2t)i+(2t^{2})j\right\}(m) \]


    \[ x=2t \]


    \[ \Rightarrow V_{x}=\dfrac{dx}{dt}=2 \]


    \[ y=2t^{2} \]


    \[ \Rightarrow V_{y}=\dfrac{dy}{dt}=4t \]


    \[ \tan\theta=\dfrac{V_{y}}{V_{x}} \]


    \[ \Rightarrow \dfrac{4t}{2}=2t \]


    By differentiating with respect to time


    \[ \dfrac{d}{dt}(\tan\theta)=\dfrac{d}{dt}(2t) \]


    \[ \Rightarrow (\sec^{2}\theta)\dfrac{d\theta}{dt}=2 \]


    \[ \Rightarrow (1+\tan^{2}\theta)\dfrac{d\theta}{dt}=2 \]


    as we know from above

    \[ \tan\theta=2t \]


    \[ \Rightarrow (1+4t^{2})\dfrac{d\theta}{dt}=2 \]


    \[ \Rightarrow \dfrac{d\theta}{dt}=\dfrac{2}{1+4t^{2}} \]


    At

    \[ t=2\sec \]


    \[ \Rightarrow \dfrac{d\theta}{dt}=\dfrac{2}{1+4\times 4}\]


    \[=\dfrac{2}{17}\ rad/s \]
