• Given:
    A long horizontal rod has a bead which can slide along its length, and initially placed at a distance L from one end A of the rod.

    • The rod is set in angular motion about A with constant angular acceleration α. If the coefficient of friction between the rod and the bead is μ and gravity is neglected, To find the time after which he bead start slipping

    Solution:


    Assuming the given motion takes place.


    The bead will be sliding when centripetal force is equal to the limiting friction.


    \[mw^2L=f_{(max)}\\\]


    \[\implies mw^2L=\mu N...........(i)\]


    Normal reaction is provided by


    \[ F(t)=mL\alpha=N.........(ii)\ \]

    as

    \[a=\alpha\]


    Now at time t


    \[ w=0+\alpha t\\ \]


    \[ \implies w=\alpha t \]


    Substituting the value of N in eqn(i), we get


    \[ mw^2L=\mu mL\alpha\\ \]


    \[ \implies (\alpha t)^2=\mu \alpha\\ \]


    \[ \implies t^2=\dfrac \mu \alpha\\ \]


    \[ \implies t=\sqrt {\dfrac \mu \alpha} \]


    is the time after which he bead start slipping
