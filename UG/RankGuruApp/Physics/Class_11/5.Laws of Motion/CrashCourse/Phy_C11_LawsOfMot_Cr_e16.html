Given,


    \[ m=7500, \]

    \[ v=800 \]


    \[ a=10 \ m/l^2 \]


    We know,

    Thrust

    \[ =v\dfrac{dm}{dt} \]

    ;


    \[ v= \]

    exhaust speed


    \[ m= \]

    amount of gas ejected


    The upward force can also be found as,


    \[ F=m(a+g) \]


    \[a=\]

    acceleration of rocket


    \[g=\]

    acceleration due to gravity


    \[=9.8m/s^2\]


    \[\therefore\]

    Thrust

    \[=F\]


    \[v\dfrac{dm}{dt}=m(a+g)\]

    ......(1)


    Substituting the given values in equation

    \[(1)\]

    , we get


    \[800\times \dfrac{dm}{dt}=7500\times (10+9.8)\]


    \[\Rightarrow \dfrac{dm}{dt}=185.625\]


    But, if we take

    \[g=10\]

    m/s


    We get

    \[\dfrac{dm}{dt}=187.5\]

    kg/s


    \[\therefore\]

    The amount of gas ejected for providing thrust


    \[\dfrac{dm}{dt}=187.5\]

    kg/s.
