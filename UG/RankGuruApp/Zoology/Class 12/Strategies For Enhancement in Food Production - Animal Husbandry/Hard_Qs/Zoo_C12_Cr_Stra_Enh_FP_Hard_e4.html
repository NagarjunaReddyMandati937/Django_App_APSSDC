• Plants obtain minerals from the soil.

    • Hence, they can be grown in a soil less nutrient medium.

    • Various nutrient media are also used to culture plant through micropropagation.

    • Because plant cells are totipotent, hence they can be propagated through micropropagation.

  • Micropropagation is a technique used in plant tissue culture in which a small plant tissue (called as explant) of a plant can be used to obtain multiple new plants, i.e., propagate the mother plant.

  • Murashige and Skoog medium or (MS Medium) is a plant growth medium used in the laboratories for the cultivation of the plant in cell culture.

    • So, the correct answer is 'Raising plants through micropropagation'.
