• The reproductive cycle or the menstrual cycle is of about 28 days and occur in three phases which are menstrual phase, proliferative phase and secretory phase.

  • Proliferative phase is regulated by the secretion of hormones from the anterior pituitary and this stimulates ovarian follicle to secrete oestrogen.

    • By the second week, most of the developing follicles die and one follicle continues to grow which mature and forms the Graafian follicle.

    • Ovulation occurs on the 14th day of the cycle and egg is released from the Graafian follicle.

  • The secretory phase is marked by a yellow coloured body known as Corpus luteum.

    • In the presence of pregnancy, human chorionic Gonadotropin (hCG) is secreted from the corpus luteum.

  • In the absence of pregnancy, corpus luteum regresses to corpus albicans and menstruation starts which marks the menstrual phase.

  • Hence, when pregnancy does not occur, life of corpus luteum is about 14 days.

    • So, the correct answer is '14'.
