• Parturition is a phenomenon of giving birth.

    • It is a process of delivering a child from the womb to the outside.

    • It is usually initiated by the release of oxytocin hormone.

  • This hormone increases the contraction of uterine muscles.

    • Prostaglandins are synthesized and released in response to the oxytocin hormone.

    • Also, an increase in estrogen and progesterone ratio indicates the initiation of the parturition process.

  • Prolactin hormones are released after the parturition in order to secrete the milk from the mammary gland.

    • Thus, the correct answer is 'Release of prolactin'.
