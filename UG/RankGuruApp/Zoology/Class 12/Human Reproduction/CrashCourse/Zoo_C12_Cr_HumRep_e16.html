• The human placenta is both metadiscoidal and monodiscoidal.

  • Discoidal refers to the structure of the placenta and mono meaning there is only one disc.

  • Metadiscoidal refers to the way the developing placenta becomes discoidal- the villi are scattered at first and then restricted to one disc, that means the placenta becomes mono-discoidal.

    • So, the correct answer is 'Discoidal'.
