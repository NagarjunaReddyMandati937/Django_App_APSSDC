• During the development of foetus, many oogonia are produced which undergo mitosis and form primary oocytes.

  • These primary oocytes remain arrested at prophase I of meiosis I throughout the childhood.

  • It is estimated that about 2 million primary oocytes are present in a female at the time of birth.

  • No primary oocyte is added after birth.

  • Hence, oogonia are constant in number as they are formed at the embryonic stages of the foetus and no oogonia are formed after birth.

    • So, the correct answer is 'Constant in number and never be added after birth'.
