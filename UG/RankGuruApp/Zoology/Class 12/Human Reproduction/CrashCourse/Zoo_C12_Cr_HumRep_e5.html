• Lactational amenorrhea is the temporary postnatal (immediate after childbirth) infertility that occurs when a woman is amenorrheic (not menstruating) and fully breastfeeding.

  • Breastfeeding can suppress ovulation and thus serve as a form of birth control.

  • During the breastfeed the same hormones that make milk, called prolactin, also suppress the release of hormones that cause eggs to mature and become fertile.

  • There is no menstruation during lactation amenorrhea because there is no ovulation due to increase in levels of prolactin.
