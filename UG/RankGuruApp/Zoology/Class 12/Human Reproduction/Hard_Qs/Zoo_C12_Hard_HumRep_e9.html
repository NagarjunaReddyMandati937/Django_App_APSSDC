• Ovulation is the release of a mature egg from the female ovary that enables the egg to be fertilized by the male sperm cells.

  • Primary oocytes are formed prenatally and remain suspended in prophase I of meiosis I for years until the onset of puberty.

  • During ovulation, primary oocyte completes meiosis I as its follicle matures.

  • One of the division product is eliminated as the polar body, and the other, secondary oocyte stays in metaphase II throughout ovulation until fertilization.

    • So the correct option is 'Secondary oocyte at Metaphase II of Meiosis II'.
