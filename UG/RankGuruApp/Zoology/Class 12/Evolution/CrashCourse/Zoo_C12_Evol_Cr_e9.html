• Convergent evolution is the presence of similar features in animals of different lineages.

  • The structures in these animals have similar functions but absent in the common ancestors and hence they are analogous structures and not homologous.

  • The Tasmanian wolf and the American wolf have several morphological similarities but they have unrelated ancestral lineages.

  • Hence, they show convergent evolution. Such convergence of functional, morphological and structural traits from phylogenetically unrelated species may be a result of random mutation due to selection pressure.

    • Thus, the correct answer is 'Convergent evolution'.
