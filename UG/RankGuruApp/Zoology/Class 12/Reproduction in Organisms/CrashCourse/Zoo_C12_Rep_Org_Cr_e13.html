• Asexual reproduction is a method of reproduction in which offspring develops from a single parent.

  • It does not involve fusion of gametes.

  • There are various methods of asexual reproduction such as budding, spore formation, binary fission, division, fragmentation, vegetative propagation, sporogenesis.

  • In fission, the content of cell are replicated internally and then are subjected to division to form two equal daughter cells.

  • In budding, a bud is formed at a certain point on parent.

  • It grows fully and then eventually break away from the parent organism.

  • In vegetative propagation, the new plant is formed from vegetative part such as tuber, bulb, sucker.

  • In sporogenesis, the new individual is formed from a spore.

  • In fragmentation, a new organism grows from a fragment of the parent.

    • Thus, the correct answer is 'All of the above'.
