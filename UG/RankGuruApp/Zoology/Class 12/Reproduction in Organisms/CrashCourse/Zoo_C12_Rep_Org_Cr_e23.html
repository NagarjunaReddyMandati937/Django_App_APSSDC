• In women, both sex chromosomes are the same and are therefore called as XX. In males, they are different and called as XY.

  • When sperm and eggs (ova) are made during meiosis they separate to form different gametes.

  • As women have XX, their eggs always carry the X chromosomes.

  • As males have XY, approximately half their sperm carry the X chromosome while the remainder carries the Y chromosome.
