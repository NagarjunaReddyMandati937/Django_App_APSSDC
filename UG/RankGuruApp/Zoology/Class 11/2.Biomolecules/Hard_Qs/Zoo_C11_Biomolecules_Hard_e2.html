• Starch is the stored form of sugar in plants, a homopolymer of glucose units exist in two forms:

    • amylose which is the helical form of a structure composed of α1-4 glycosidic linkages, and amylopectin has a structure like glycogen except that α 1- 6 glycosidic linkage present only at branches and amylose in starch is responsible for the formation of deep blue colour in the presence of iodine as it fits into the helix structure whereas Glycogen is the storage form of sugar in humans and other vertebrates and more highly branched structure than amylopectin but lacks α 1-6 bonding.

  • Glycogen is usually stored in liver and muscle cells whereas starch stored in the amyloplast cells in plants.
