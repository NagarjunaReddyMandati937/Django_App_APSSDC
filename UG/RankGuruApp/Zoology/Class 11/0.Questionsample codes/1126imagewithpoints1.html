• Geostrophic Wind is Horizontal wind in the upper atmosphere that moves parallel to isobars.

  • Results from a balance between pressure gradient force and Coriolis force.

  • Anabatic Winds are upslope winds driven by warmer surface temperatures on a mountain slope than the surrounding air column.

  • Cyclonic circulation is the swirling motion of winds caused by a low-pressure area.

  • Jet streams are relatively narrow bands of strong wind in the upper levels of the atmosphere.

  • The winds blow from west to east in jet streams but the flow often shifts to the north and south.
