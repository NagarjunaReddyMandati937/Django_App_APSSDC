• The liquidity trap is the situation in which the current interest rates are low and savings rates are high, rendering monetary policy ineffective.

  • In a liquidity trap, consumers choose to avoid bonds and keep their funds in savings because of the prevailing belief that interest rates will soon rise.

  • A liquidity trap is caused when people hoard cash because they expect an adverse event such as deflation.

  • Attempts by policymakers to push market consumption by increasing the money supply doesn't yield results.

  • It results in low consumer spending, overall demand in the economy suffers.

  • Thus production activities suffer leading to near stagnant economic growth.
