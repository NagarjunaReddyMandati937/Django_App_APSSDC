  • B. Pneumatophores or respiratory roots (e.g. Avicennia, Rhizophora, Heritiera)-Plants growing in marshy, water-logged soils which contain almost no air, develop some branches which grow vertically upwards into the air. Such roots are provided towards the upper end with numerous pores through which gases diffuse in and out.

  • C, Haustoria or sucking roots (e.g. Cuscuta and Viscum )-Root penetrates into the tissue of the host plant and draw nutrients from the host. Such parasitic plants are not completely photosynthetic.

  • D, Stilt roots (e.g. Screwpine)-Adventitious roots arise from lower part of the main stem and grow obliquely towards the soil. They keep the plant erect by giving additional support.

  • E. Assimilatory Roots- These are green, aerial, adventitious roots which prepare food materials by photosynthesis are called photosynthetic roots or assimilatory roots e.g., Taeniophyllum, Trapa and Tinospora.
