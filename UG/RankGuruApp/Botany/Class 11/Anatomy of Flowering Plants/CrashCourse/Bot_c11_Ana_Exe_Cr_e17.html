• In a young dicot stem, the cambium is single layered.

  • It is made by the intrafascicular cambium present within the vascular bundles and the dedifferentiation of the parenchyma cells of the medullary rays between the vascular bundles, called interfascicular cambium.

  • Their combination gives a circular cambium that produces xylem elements on the inner side and the phloem elements on the outer side.

    • Hence, the correct answer is 'Single layered'.
