• Stomata are pores found on the surface of leaves.

  • These pores help in exchange of gases from the environment and also in transpiration.

  • Lenticels are pores found on bark of woody plants which allow gaseous exchange between atmosphere and internal tissues.

  • At night stomata are closed and soil moisture content is high.

  • This leads to high root pressure which leads to water accumulation in plants.

  • This excess sap leaks from the tip of leaves and this process of exudation of xylem sap is known as guttation.

  • Guttation pores are not aerating and do not allow gaseous exchange, they are only involved in getting rid of excess water.

    • Thus the correct answer is B.
