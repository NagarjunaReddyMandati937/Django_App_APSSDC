• Lateral meristems are usually found in woody plants and consist of vascular and cork cambium.

  • They produce secondary tissues in the roots and stem from the vascular cambium.

  • The cork cambium mainly replaces the epidermis in the roots and stems and thus is responsible for the secondary growth in these parts.

  • Interfascicular meristem is a secondary meristem and during secondary growth, its derivatives are secondary phloem, secondary xylem, and conjunctive tissue.

    • So, the correct answer is 'All of the above'.
