• Fungi are divided into two groups, ascomycetes and basidiomycetes depending on their sexual spores.

  • In the life cycle of ascomycetes and basidiomycetes cells in fruiting bodies are monkaryotic and diploid.

  • In ascomycetes, the spores are produced within asci.

  • Asci may be cylindrical or spherical in shape. Each ascus contains eight spores.

  • It is monokaryotic.

  • In basidiomycetes, the spores develop on projections that grow out from basidia.

  • Basidia are elongated and club-like in shape.

  • They are diploid.

  • Each basidium has four projections and four spores.

  • It has no dividing walls (or septa).

    • Thus, the correct answer is 'Monokaryotic and diploid (Ascus /Basidium).'
