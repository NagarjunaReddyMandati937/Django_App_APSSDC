• Ascomycetes are also called as sac fungi.

  • Examples include brewer's yeast, baker's yeast, dead man's fingers, cup fungi.

  • The most important characteristic of these group of fungi is they produce ascospores.

  • They produce 8 ascospores inside a sac like ascus.

  • They reproduce sexually using ascospores.

  • They also reproduce asexually by conidia.

  • Conidia are also called as mitospores.

  • These are produced on specialized stalks called conidiophores.

  • The mycelium of these fungi is made up of septate hyphae and it is branched.

    • Thus, the correct answer is 'Septate branched mycelium.'
