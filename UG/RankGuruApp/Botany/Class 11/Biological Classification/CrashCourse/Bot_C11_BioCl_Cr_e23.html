Characteristics of Chrysophyta -

  • Chrysophyta is also known as golden brown algae.

  • They are considered as the primary source of food for zooplankton.

  • Brown algae show photoautotrophic nutrition.

  • Brown algae are unicellular marine or freshwater protists.

  • In some species of diatoms, a specialised cell called auxospore are produced during their life cycle.

  • Auxospore plays important role in growth, sexual reproduction and dormancy.

  • Diatoms may be the pennate type (bilateral symmetry) or centric type (radial symmetry).
