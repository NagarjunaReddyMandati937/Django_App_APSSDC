• A chromosome in which the centromere is located quite near one end of the chromosome is called the acrocentric chromosome.

  • Humans normally have five pairs of acrocentric chromosomes.

  • Down syndrome is caused by an extra acrocentric chromosome (chromosome21).

  • The acrocentric chromosome does not provide the attachment site for spindle fibers because centromere is located quite near one end of the chromosome.

So, the correct answer is 'Acrocentric'.
