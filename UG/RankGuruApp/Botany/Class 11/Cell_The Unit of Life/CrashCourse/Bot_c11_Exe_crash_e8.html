• Somatic nucleated cells are totipotent or able to produce a complete organism.

  • This is especially so, in the case of plant cells.

  • In animal systems, nerve cells or neurons lose their capacity to divide.

  • Also, a fully differentiated kidney cell may divide to form a kidney cell only and not a complete organism because of differentiation.

  • In-plant systems, sclerenchyma are dead at maturity and hence, do not divide.

  • A parenchyma cell is normally a non-dividing permanent cell but can be suitably induced to divide and form a complete organism, that is, under certain conditions it can dedifferentiate and revert back to meristematic activity.

  • Thus, the correct answer is option B.
