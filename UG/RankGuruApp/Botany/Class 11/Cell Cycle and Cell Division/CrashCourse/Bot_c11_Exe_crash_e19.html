• In sexual reproduction, an egg cell from female and a sperm from a male fuse to form a zygote, precursor to an embryo offspring.

  • The offspring is generally distinct from parents.

  • Both the gametes contain half the number of required chromosomes, that is they are haploid in nature.

  • The zygote contains the exact number of chromosomes required.

  • This is how chromosome number is conserved from generation to generation.

  • The zygote undergoes further cell division to form an embryo.

    • So, the correct answer is the zygote.
