There are certain checkpoints in a cell cycle at which it is decided whether the cell should move forward with the division or not. These checkpoints are as follows:

  • Restriction point or G1 checkpoint- At this checkpoint, it is decided whether cellular conditions are favorable or not.

    • Here, the cell size and proteins are checked.

    • After this checkpoint is cleared, cell progresses to the S-phase.

  • G2 checkpoint- At this point, it is assessed whether the DNA replication has occurred accurately.

    • Any error in replication would arrest the cell division progress at this checkpoint.

  • M checkpoint- This occurs at the metaphase, and in here it is checked whether all the chromosomes are properly aligned to the spindle fibers or not because right after the metaphase, chromosomes have to be distributed to the daughter cells and if they are not aligned properly, the faulty distribution might occur.

    • Any problem at this stage woul arrest the cell cycle.

  • So, the cell cycle is completed when all the checkpoints are successfully crossed.

    • Hence, the correct answer is 'When all checkpoints are successfully crossed'.
