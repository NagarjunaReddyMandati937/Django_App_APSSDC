• G1 phase comes after S phase.

  • In S phase synthesis of DNA occurs.

  • The amount of DNA doubles but the chromosome number or diploid content of the cell remains the same.

  • So, if the mother cell has 32 picogram DNA in G2 , this means originally the cell has 16 picogram of DNA.

  • The mother cell which forms the pollen grain is diploid and undergo meiosis to produce haploid pollen grains.

  • So, if the diploid mother cell has originally 16 picogram of DNA, the amount of DNA in haploid pollen grain would be equal to half of 16 i.e. 8 picogram.

    • So, the correct answer is '8 pg'.
