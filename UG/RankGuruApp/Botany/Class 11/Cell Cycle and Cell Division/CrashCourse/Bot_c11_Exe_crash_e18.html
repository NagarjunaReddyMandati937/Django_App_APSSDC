• Crossing over or recombination occurs during pachytene. Recombination involves mutual exchange of the corresponding segments of non-sister chromatids of homologous chromosomes.

  • It takes place by breakage and reunion of chromatid segments.

  • Breakage, called nicking, is assisted by an enzyme endonuclease and reunion, termed annealing, is aided by an enzyme ligase.

  • Crossing over leads to recombination of genetic material on the two chromosomes.
