• In anaphase, chromosomes are arranged on the equatorial plate for a short period.

  • In anaphase centromeres of chromosomes start to divide into two, forming daughter chromatids with centromere in each.

  • Daughter chromosomes are repulsive so, migrate towards opposite poles.

  • Spindle fibres attached to the centromeres shorten and pull the chromosomes to the poles.

  • In anaphasic movement of chromosomes, the centromeres lead the path while the limbs trail behind. So anaphasic chromosomes appear as V-, L-, J- and I-shaped.
