Let

    \[ P(E)=a,\ P(F)=b \]


    Given that:


    Probability that both

    \[ E \]

    and

    \[ F, \]


    \[ ab=\dfrac{1}{6} \]

    ......(1)


    \[ b=\dfrac{1}{6a} \]


    Probability that neither

    \[ E \]

    nor

    \[ F \]

    happens,


    \[ (1-a)(1-b)=\dfrac{1}{3}, \]


    \[ 3+3ab-3a-3b=1, \]


    \[ 4+1-6a-6b=0, \]

    [From equation(1)]


    \[ 6a+6b=5, \]


    On substituting the value of

    \[ b, \]

    we get


    \[ 6a^2-5a+1=0, \]


    \[ 6a^2-2a-3a+1=0, \]


    \[ 2a(3a-1)-1(3a-1)=0, \]


    \[ (2a-1)(3a-1)=0, \]


    So,


    \[ a=\dfrac{1}{3}\ or\ \dfrac{1}{2}. \]


    and


    \[ b=\dfrac{1}{2}\ or\ \dfrac{1}{3} \]


    Hence, this is the required result.
