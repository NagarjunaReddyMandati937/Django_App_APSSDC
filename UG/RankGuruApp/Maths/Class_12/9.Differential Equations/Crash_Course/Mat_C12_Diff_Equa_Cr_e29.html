The given differential equation is,



    \[ \left(1+e^{\dfrac{x}{y}}\right)dx+e^{\dfrac{x}{y}}\left(1-\dfrac{x}{y}\right)dy=0 \]


    \[ \Rightarrow \dfrac{dx}{dy}=\dfrac{e^{\dfrac{x}{y}}\left(\dfrac{x}{y}-1\right)} {1+e^{\dfrac{x}{y}}} \]

    ------- ( 1 )



    \[ \Rightarrow \]

    Let

    \[ x=vy \]


    \[ \Rightarrow \dfrac{dx}{dy}=v+y\dfrac{dv}{dy} \]


    \[ v+y\dfrac{dv}{dy}=\dfrac{e^v(v-1)}{1+e^v} \]


    \[ \Rightarrow y\dfrac{dv}{dy}=\dfrac{ve^v-e^v}{1+e^v}-v \]


    \[ \Rightarrow y\dfrac{dv}{dy}=\dfrac{ve^v-e^v-v-ve^v}{1+e^v} \]


    \[ \Rightarrow y\dfrac{dv}{dy}=\dfrac{-(v+e^v)}{(1+e^v)} \]


    \[ \Rightarrow \dfrac{(1+e^v)}{(v+e^v)}dv=-\dfrac{dy}{y} \]


    Integrating both sides, we get


    \[ \int \dfrac{(1+e^v)}{(v+e^v)}dv=-\int \dfrac{dy}{y} \]

    ------ ( 2 )


    Let

    \[ I=\int\dfrac{(1+e^v)}{(v+e^v)}dv \]


    Put

    \[ v+e^v=t \]


    \[ (1+e^v)dv=dt \]


    So,

    \[ I=\int\dfrac{dt}{t}=\log\,t=\log\,(v+e^v) \]


    So, from ( 2 ), we get


    \[ \log(v+e^v)=-\log\,y+\log\,C \]


    \[ \Rightarrow \log\left(\dfrac{x}{y}+e^{\dfrac{x}{y}}\right)+\log\,y=\log\,C \]


    \[ \Rightarrow \log\left[y\left(\dfrac{x}{y}+e^{\dfrac{x}{y}}\right)\right]=\log\,C \]


    \[ \Rightarrow y\left(\dfrac{x}{y}+e^{\dfrac{x}{y}}\right)=C \]


    \[ \Rightarrow x+ye^{\dfrac{x}{y}}=C \]
