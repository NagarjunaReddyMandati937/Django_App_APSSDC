\( e^{4x} + e^{3x} - 4e^{2x} + e^x + 1 = 0 \)



    Let \( e^x = t \in (0, \infty) \)



    So equation becomes,



    \( t^4 + t^3 - 4t^2 + t + 1 = 0 \)



    \( t^2 + t - 4 + \dfrac{1}{t} + \dfrac{1}{t^2} = 0 \)



    \( \left(t^2 + \dfrac{1}{t^2} \right) + \left(t + \dfrac{1}{t} \right) - 4 = 0 \)



    \( \left(t + \dfrac{1}{t} \right) = \alpha , \,\, \alpha > 0 \)



    \( (\alpha^2 + \alpha - 2 - 4) = 0 \)



    \( (\alpha^2 - 2) + (\alpha - 4) = 0 \)



    \( \alpha^2 + \alpha - 6 = 0 \)



    \( \alpha^2 + 3 \alpha - 2 \alpha - 6 = 0 \)



    \( \alpha (\alpha + 3) - 2 ( \alpha + 3) = 0 \)



    \( (\alpha + 3) (\alpha - 2) = 0 \)



    \( \alpha = 2, -3 \)



    So, \( \alpha = 2 , \alpha \neq -3 [\because \alpha > 0]\)



    \( e^x + e^{-x} = 2 \)



    \( e^{2x}-2e^x+1=0 \)



    \( e^x=1 \)



    \( \therefore x = 0 \) only solution.



    only one real Root.
